I For Work

A home-services-on-demand app that makes a difference.

What is I For Work?

I For Work is a mobile application available for both iOS and Android that will allow users to request for home services such as cleaning, gardening, painting, etc. All you have to do is select what you want done, give some deatils about the job, include your location, price range and preffered date and time and send in a request. Experienced service providers in your area will see your request and reply with a quote and then you can decide who to choose. You'll also be able to communicate with your service provider to ensure the job gets done smoothly.

What makes us different?

Newly-arrived people from culturally and linguistically diverse (CALD) backgrounds are having difficulties in finding employment even for low skilled work due to obstacles such as low English proficiency and discrimination from employers; hence they are more likely to start a small business of their own. This project aims to provide such sole trader business and individuals a platform where they can register and offer their services and for potential customers to be able to find and book their services. I For Work will:

  • Facilitate connections between CLAD backgrounds and established communities in the Illawarra.
  • Reduce social isolation
  • Increase employment opportunities
  • Foster independence and entrepreneurship in CALD communities

Who is behind I For Work?

Illawarra Multicultural Services Inc (IMS) is the lead agency in the Illawarra and Shoalhaven areas providing a range of services to migrants, refugees and the mainstream community since 1980. IMS began as the Illawarra Migrant Resource Centre and is one of the longest standing centres providing assistance and support to newly arrived migrant and refugee communities. IMS receives funding from both Commonwealth and State governments to provide a range of services. IMS recognises that Australia is a multicultural society and as such it values equal participation and equitable access to services and programs. IMS is also committed to social justice principles.

Target Market

The application is targeted towards two specific groups of people Service Providers and Customers. Currently, the application will be marketed in the Illawarra region; however, in the future if the application is successful and the demand for it is high, it will be marketed to a wider audience in other parts of Australia.

Service Providers

Service providers are defined as those individuals and businesses who will register into the application to offer their services, the registration process for service providers includes a vetting process conducted by IMS. The application aims to mainly target refugee/migrant background people to register as service providers. In the Wollongong area, Middle Eastern communities constitute majority of refugee arrivals, with Syria being where the greatest portion of refugees originate from. Most other humanitarian entrants originate from Congo, Eritrea, and Burma. The most widely spoken languages among refugees since 2011 is Arabic, not all of the Burmese people in Wollongong speak the Burmese language, they are mainly from the Karenni, Karen, Kachin and Chin hill tribes and each have their own distinct language. Swahili, French or Ewe are usually spoken by the people who originate from African regions. Among newly arrived refugees and migrants, the level of educational ability and skill they possess varies. Some have strong English proficiency and tertiary qualifications but fail to secure employment as they do not have any Australian work experience and/or their qualifications are not recognised in Australia, others do not have such qualifications and speak little to no English which makes it difficult for them to communicate with the general community and also can lead to discrimination.

Customers

The customers of this application are defined as individuals from the general community, mostly around the Illawarra region, who are seeking services such as lawn mowing, cleaning, gardening, carpet laying and removal, etc. Customers can directly register into the application to browse and request services. In the case of customers application will be marketed towards people aged 20 and above, mainly homeowners and other people that may require home services on demand. Students may make up a small percentage of customers who may want cost effective service options.

To cater to our target audience who may have low english proficiency, we have a translate option at the top right corner of the webpage so that they are able to view this website in the language they are most comfortable in. We also provide the User Manual in Arabic and Swahili.

Design Methodology

Our clients have expressed that they would like to stay true to the design ideologies of both Android and iOS which is why we will follow the material design principles for the Android version of the app and flat design for the iOS version of the app. Flat design is great for users who are very familiar with digital interaction, material design may look flat but it takes the Z-axis into consideration making it multidimensional. Both flat and material are minimalist styles, the application will use minimalism to ensure only important and useful functionalities/information are readily available to the user.

The colour scheme of the application has been selected to have a similar green theme as the IMS logo but not the exact same green to give I For Work its own brand colours but to still have a link to the IMS branding. High contrast makes imagery easier to see, colour contrast is used for users to be able to distinguish various text and non-text elements, a low-contrast image may be hard for some users to see in bright or low light conditions. The application incorporates lots of icons that enable users to readily recognise their meaning, icons are assumed to be easier to learn and remember than commands which is helpful for users with low English proficiency. Moreover, the application will have language settings allow users to change the default language to a language of their choice hence the application is designed with sufficient space for large and foreign language fonts.

The application has both a flat list menu and an expanding menu, flat list menus are good at displaying a small number of options at the same time and where the size of the display is small, and an expanding menu enables more options to be shown on a single screen than is possible with a single flat menu. Some more design features include: Back buttons which gives users control and freedom to easily escape from places they unexpectedly find themselves, by using clearly marked emergency exits', error messages that helps users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors, plain language is used to describe the nature of the problem and suggest a way of solving it, acknowledging actions provide text to let the user know whether an action they chose has been completed and confirming actions ask the user to verify that they want to proceed with an action.

Development Environment

Android Studio: Android Studio is the official integrated development environment (IDE) for Android application development. It is based on the IntelliJ IDEA, a Java integrated development environment for software, and integrates its code editing and developer tools. To support application development within the Android operating system, Android Studio uses a Gradle-based build system, emulator, code templates, and Github integration. Every project in Android Studio has one or more modalities with source code and resource files. These modalities include Android app modules, Library modules, and Google App Engine modules.

Xcode: Xcode is an integrated development environment (IDE) for macOS containing a suite of software development tools developed by Apple for developing software for iOS. The Xcode suite includes most of Apple's developer documentation, and built-in Interface Builder, an application used to construct graphical user interfaces. Xcode lets users take advantage of SwiftUI which is an innovative and simple way to build user interfaces across all Apple platforms using Swift. One can build user interfaces for any Apple device using just one set of tools and APIs and with declarative Swift syntax to help write simpler code that clearly states what your user interface should do.
